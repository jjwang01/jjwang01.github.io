Justin Wang

I am an undergraduate student and aspiring physician data-scientist studying Computer Science at Stanford University. My main interests lie in the application of deep learning to genomic data, data analytics to clinical studies, and computer vision to surgical techniques.

At the Kundaje Lab my projects include ChromDragonn, a convolutional neural network architecture designed to predict accessible, likely transcribed, regions within the genome using DNA sequence and gene expression profiles, and PrimateAI, a framework for identifying and predicting rare non-Mendelian genetic disorders from amino acid sequence in proteins.

At Siemens, I developed an application called TrimTab for Siemens' trademark CAD/CAM software, NX. TrimTab enables users to piece together designs in a logical piece-by-piece manner rather than using esoteric commands traditionally found in CAD software, making it simpler to create patterned generative designs.

In the Stanford Diabetics Research Center, I've created a pipeline to parse raw CSV data gathered from closed loop insulin delivery systems into metrics useful for determining the efficacy of different insulins for Type I Diabetes patients.
